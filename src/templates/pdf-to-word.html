How to convert a PDF to Word

Click the box above to choose a PDF, or drag one straight in. Then click Convert to Word. Your browser reads the text out of the PDF, rebuilds it as an editable .docx, and a download link appears for the Word file — usually in a second or two.

It works best on PDFs that hold real text — the kind exported from a word processor, browser, or report tool. It keeps the words, paragraphs, and headings; very complex multi-column layouts and tables may need a quick tidy-up in Word. Scanned documents are pictures of pages with no underlying text, so there is nothing to pull out.

About your privacy: the default conversion runs entirely in your browser, so your PDF never leaves your device. If you choose the optional higher-fidelity server conversion, that file is uploaded over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection, converted, and immediately discarded — nothing is stored, logged, or shared.

Frequently asked questions

Is this PDF to Word converter free?

Yes — completely free, with no signup and no watermark on the result.

Are my files uploaded anywhere?

The default in-browser conversion uploads nothing — your PDF stays on your device. There is also an optional "server conversion" for higher fidelity; that one uploads your PDF over an encrypted connection, converts it, and discards it right after.

Will my formatting be kept?

It rebuilds the text, paragraphs, and headings from text-based PDFs. Because it runs privately in your browser, very complex layouts and tables may need a little cleanup in Word.

Does it work on scanned PDFs?

No. Scanned PDFs are images with no underlying text, so there is nothing to extract. Use a PDF exported from a program for the best result.

Is there a file size limit?

The limit is 50 MB per PDF, which covers almost any text document. Very large files take a little longer because the work happens on your device.
