Six calendar months vs the 182.5-day midpoint

There are two reasonable ways to define a half birthday, and they usually land a day or two apart. The calendar method — what most people mean — simply adds six months to your birthday and keeps the same day number. It's easy to remember and always falls on the same date each year, which is why this calculator uses it for the main answer.

The 182.5-day midpoint method adds exactly half of a 365-day year to your birthday. Because months have different lengths, the true halfway point can be slightly before or after the six-month calendar date. Neither is more "correct" — the calendar date is friendlier for planning, while the midpoint is the literal middle of your personal year. The calculator shows both so you can pick whichever you like.

Why some dates get clamped. Adding six months to a late-in-the-month birthday can produce a date that doesn't exist — 31 August plus six months would be 31 February. In those cases the half birthday is moved back to the last valid day of the target month (28 or 29 February), exactly the way calendars and reminder apps handle impossible dates. February 29 birthdays and other edge cases are handled the same consistent way.

Common half birthday questions

What is a half birthday? It's the day halfway through the year from your birthday — usually the same date six calendar months later. A 14 March birthday gives a half birthday of 14 September.

How do you calculate a half birthday? Add six calendar months to your birthday. If the resulting day doesn't exist, it's clamped to the last day of that month (for example 31 August becomes 28 or 29 February).

What is the 182.5-day method? Instead of six calendar months, it adds exactly half a year — 182.5 days — to your birthday. It can differ from the calendar date by a day or two, and the calculator shows it as a secondary readout.

What if my birthday is on August 31? Six months later would be 31 February, which isn't a real date, so your half birthday is clamped to 28 February (or 29 February in a leap year).

How many days until my next half birthday? The countdown counts whole days from today — using your device's clock — to your next half birthday, rolling forward to next year automatically if this year's has already passed.

When is my half birthday this year? It's the same date six calendar months after your birthday, so it falls on a fixed date every year — a 14 March birthday is always 14 September. Enter your birth date to see this year's date, weekday, and countdown.
