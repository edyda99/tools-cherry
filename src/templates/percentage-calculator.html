How to use the percentage calculator

Each of the three calculators works on its own — fill in both numbers and the answer updates as you type. The first answers "what is X% of Y", which is handy for discounts, tips, sales tax, or commission. The second answers "X is what percent of Y", useful for test scores, completion rates, or working out a share of a total. The third gives the percentage increase or decrease from one value to another — for price changes, growth, weight loss, or any before-and-after comparison.

The percentage formulas

To find a percentage of a number, multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100: P% of N = N × P ÷ 100. So 20% of 50 is 50 × 20 ÷ 100 = 10. To find what percent one number is of another, divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100: X ÷ Y × 100. To find a percentage change, subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original value, and multiply by 100: (new − old) ÷ old × 100; a negative result means the value went down.

A quick mental shortcut: 10% of any number is that number with the decimal point moved one place to the left, so 10% of 80 is 8. From there you can double it for 20%, halve it for 5%, or add a 10% and a 5% chunk for 15%.
