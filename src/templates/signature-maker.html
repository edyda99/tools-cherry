How to make a signature

Draw mode: sign on the pad with your mouse, finger, or a stylus. Pick a pen color and thickness, and use Clear to start over. Type mode: type your name and choose a handwriting-style look, color, and size.

When you download, the image is automatically trimmed to your signature and saved as a PNG with a transparent background — so it drops cleanly onto documents, contracts, photos, or web pages without a white box around it.

Your signature never leaves your device — it is created entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded.

Not a legal e-signature. This tool makes a quick signature image for everyday use. It does not sign, bind, timestamp, or verify a document the way a legal electronic-signature service does. For anything legally binding, use a proper e-signature provider.

Frequently asked questions

Is my signature uploaded anywhere? No. It is created on your device, in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server.

What format is the download? A transparent PNG, trimmed to your signature.

How do I add it to a Word document or PDF? Insert the PNG as a picture where you want to sign (Insert → Picture in Word, or the image/stamp tool in a PDF editor). The transparent background means only your signature shows.

Should I draw or type it? Draw mode looks the most natural, especially with a finger or stylus. Type mode is quick and tidy when you just need a readable handwriting-style signature.

Will it look crisp? Yes — the drawing pad adapts to high-resolution (Retina) screens so lines stay sharp on screen and in print.
