How it works, and what it keeps

This tool converts the content and structure of your document rather than taking a pixel-perfect photograph of Word's layout. That trade-off is deliberate: it means the text in the finished PDF stays real and selectable (you can copy from it and search it), instead of being flattened into an image.

Here is an honest picture of what survives the conversion and what may not:

  • Kept: paragraph text, heading levels, bulleted and numbered lists, and images embedded in the document. Text stays selectable and searchable in the PDF.
  • Simplified: tables are converted to plain text rows (cells separated by " | "), not a drawn grid. Bold or italic applied to a few words inside a paragraph is not carried through — only whole headings are shown in bold.
  • Not reproduced: exact theme fonts (the PDF uses a standard built-in font), multi-column layouts, precise page margins, headers/footers, and page-break positions from Word. Non-Latin alphabets (for example Arabic or Chinese) may not render with the built-in font.
  • Note on edits: if the document has tracked changes, the accepted content is converted as though the changes were already applied. Comments are not included in the PDF.

For a pixel-perfect copy of a heavily designed document, open the .docx in Word or Google Docs and use their own "Export to PDF" or "Print to PDF". For everyday letters, reports, notes, and text-heavy documents, this converter produces a clean, shareable PDF without your file ever leaving your device.

Why in-browser? Word documents carry names, addresses, salary figures, contract terms, and unpublished drafts. A converter that runs entirely in your browser means that content never has to sit on someone else's server, even for a moment. Once this page has loaded, you can disconnect from the internet and it still works — the clearest possible proof that nothing is being sent anywhere.

Frequently asked questions

Is this Word to PDF converter free?

Yes — completely free, with no signup and no watermark on the result.

Are my files uploaded anywhere?

No. The conversion runs entirely in your browser, so your Word document never leaves your device. Nothing is uploaded, stored, or logged.

Will my formatting be kept?

It rebuilds the text, headings, lists, and embedded images, and the PDF's text stays real and selectable. Because it runs privately in your browser, exact fonts, multi-column layouts, and complex tables may need a little cleanup. Tables are converted to plain text rows rather than a formatted grid.

Does it work on any .docx file?

It works on standard .docx files from Word, Google Docs, or LibreOffice. Old-style .doc files are not supported — open one in Word and save it as .docx first. Password-protected documents need the password removed before converting.

Is there a file size limit?

The limit is 30 MB per document, which covers almost any Word file. Very large documents with many images take a little longer because the work happens on your device.

How is this different from other Word to PDF converters?

Most free online converters upload your file to a server before converting it. This one converts in your browser, so nothing leaves your device. It also prioritizes real, selectable PDF text over a pixel-perfect copy of Word's layout.

Can I convert Word to PDF with no internet connection?

Yes. Once the page has loaded, turn off Wi-Fi or switch to airplane mode — the conversion still works, because your document is read and rebuilt entirely on your device.

Do I need to install anything or create an account?

No. It runs in your web browser with no install, no plugin, and no signup.
